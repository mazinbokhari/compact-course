<h2 style="text-align: center;"><span style="text-decoration: underline; color: #007fff;">Charge</span></h2>
There are two types of charges, negative and positive. If two charges have the same sign, they repel each other. If they have opposite signs, they are attracted to one another

<span style="text-decoration: underline; color: #007fff;">Atom:</span> Basic structure that makes up all elements

<span style="text-decoration: underline;"><span style="color: #007fff; text-decoration: underline;">Coulomb (C):</span></span> Unit of measure for charges

<span style="text-decoration: underline; color: #007fff;">Proton:</span> Particle inside the atom that has a positive charge
<ul>
	<li>Mass: 1.6726231 × 10<sup>-27</sup> kg</li>
	<li>Mp in your unit section of the TI-89</li>
	<li>Charge: +1.6 x 10<sup>-19</sup> C</li>
</ul>
<span style="text-decoration: underline; color: #007fff;">Electron:</span> Particle inside the atom that has a negative charge
<ul>
	<li>Mass: 9.1093897 × 10<sup>-31</sup> kg</li>
	<li>Me in a calc in your unit section of the TI-89</li>
	<li>Charge: -1.6 x 10<sup>-19</sup> C</li>
</ul>
<span style="text-decoration: underline; color: #007fff;">Neutron:</span> Particle inside the atom that has a neutral charge

<ul>
	<li>Units =&gt; (N). Symbol =&gt; FE</li>
</ul>
