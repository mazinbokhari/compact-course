<h2 style="text-align: center;">Limits</h2>
<span style="text-decoration: underline; color: #007fff;">Limit:</span> The value a function approaches or equals as its x-value gets arbitrarily close to a point
<ul>
	<li><span style="line-height: 14px;">We write the limit as </span></li>
</ul>
<img class="aligncenter" alt="{\color{Blue} \lim_{x\rightarrow a}f(x)=L}" src="http://latex.codecogs.com/gif.latex?{\color{Blue}&amp;space;\lim_{x\rightarrow&amp;space;a}f(x)=L}" align="absmiddle" />
<ul>
	<li>The limit from the right side of a value is seen with a + above the value</li>
</ul>
<img class="aligncenter" alt="{\color{Blue} \lim_{x\rightarrow a^{+}}f(x)=L}" src="http://latex.codecogs.com/gif.latex?{\color{Blue}&amp;space;\lim_{x\rightarrow&amp;space;a^{+}}f(x)=L}" align="absmiddle" />
<ul>
	<li>The limit from the left side of a value is seen with a - above the value</li>
</ul>
<img class="aligncenter" alt="{\color{Blue} \lim_{x\rightarrow a^{-}}f(x)=L}" src="http://latex.codecogs.com/gif.latex?{\color{Blue}&amp;space;\lim_{x\rightarrow&amp;space;a^{-}}f(x)=L}" align="absmiddle" />

For a limit to exist, the limit from the limit from the right has to equal the limit from the left
<p style="text-align: center;"><img alt="{\color{Blue} \lim_{x\rightarrow a^{+}}f(x)=\lim_{x\rightarrow a^{-}}f(x)=L}" src="http://latex.codecogs.com/gif.latex?{\color{Blue}&amp;space;\lim_{x\rightarrow&amp;space;a^{+}}f(x)=\lim_{x\rightarrow&amp;space;a^{-}}f(x)=L}" align="absmiddle" /></p>

<h3 style="text-align: center;">Finding the Limit of a Quotient of Polynomials</h3>
If we want to find <img alt="\lim_{x \rightarrow \infty }\frac{P(x)}{Q(x)}" src="http://latex.codecogs.com/gif.latex?\lim_{x&amp;space;\rightarrow&amp;space;\infty&amp;space;}\frac{P(x)}{Q(x)}" align="absmiddle" /> if <img alt="P(x)" src="http://latex.codecogs.com/gif.latex?P(x)" align="absmiddle" /> and <img alt="Q(x)" src="http://latex.codecogs.com/gif.latex?Q(x)" align="absmiddle" /> are polynomials, we just need to divide the highest power of <img alt="P(x)" src="http://latex.codecogs.com/gif.latex?P(x)" align="absmiddle" /> by the highest power of <img alt="Q(x)" src="http://latex.codecogs.com/gif.latex?Q(x)" align="absmiddle" />.
<ul>
	<li><span style="line-height: 14px;"><span style="line-height: 14px;">If the highest powers of
<img alt="P(x)" src="http://latex.codecogs.com/gif.latex?P(x)" align="absmiddle" /> and <img alt="Q(x)" src="http://latex.codecogs.com/gif.latex?Q(x)" align="absmiddle" /> are equal, </span></span><img style="line-height: 22px;" alt="\lim_{x \rightarrow \infty }\frac{P(x)}{Q(x)}=\frac{a_{n}}{b_{n}}" src="http://latex.codecogs.com/gif.latex?\lim_{x&amp;space;\rightarrow&amp;space;\infty&amp;space;}\frac{P(x)}{Q(x)}=\frac{a_{n}}{b_{n}}" align="absmiddle" /> where <img alt="a_{n}" src="http://latex.codecogs.com/gif.latex?a_{n}" align="absmiddle" /> and <img alt="b_{n}" src="http://latex.codecogs.com/gif.latex?b_{n}" align="absmiddle" /> are the coefficients of the highest powers and the line <img style="line-height: 22px;" alt="y=\frac{a_{n}}{b_{n}}" src="http://latex.codecogs.com/gif.latex?y=\frac{a_{n}}{b_{n}}" align="absmiddle" /><span style="line-height: 22px;"> is a horizontal asymptote on the graph of </span><img style="line-height: 22px;" alt="y=\frac{P(x)}{Q(x)}" src="http://latex.codecogs.com/gif.latex?y=\frac{P(x)}{Q(x)}" align="absmiddle" />
<ul>
	<li>ex.) <img alt="{\color{Red} \lim_{x \rightarrow \infty }\frac{4x^{3}}{9x^{3}}=\frac{4}{9}}" src="http://latex.codecogs.com/gif.latex?{\color{Red}&amp;space;\lim_{x&amp;space;\rightarrow&amp;space;\infty&amp;space;}\frac{4x^{3}}{9x^{3}}=\frac{4}{9}}" align="absmiddle" /></li>
</ul>
</li>
	<li>If <img alt="P(x)" src="http://latex.codecogs.com/gif.latex?P(x)" align="absmiddle" /> has a higher power than <img alt="Q(x)" src="http://latex.codecogs.com/gif.latex?Q(x)" align="absmiddle" />, <img alt="\lim_{x \rightarrow \infty }\frac{P(x)}{Q(x)}=+\infty\, \, or\, \, -\infty" src="http://latex.codecogs.com/gif.latex?\lim_{x&amp;space;\rightarrow&amp;space;\infty&amp;space;}\frac{P(x)}{Q(x)}=+\infty\,&amp;space;\,&amp;space;or\,&amp;space;\,&amp;space;-\infty" align="absmiddle" /> and there are no asymptotes on the graph of <img alt="y=\frac{P(x)}{Q(x)}" src="http://latex.codecogs.com/gif.latex?y=\frac{P(x)}{Q(x)}" align="absmiddle" />
<ul>
	<li>ex.) <img alt="{\color{Red} \lim_{x \rightarrow \infty }\frac{4x^{4}}{9x^{3}}=\infty}" src="http://latex.codecogs.com/gif.latex?{\color{Red}&amp;space;\lim_{x&amp;space;\rightarrow&amp;space;\infty&amp;space;}\frac{4x^{4}}{9x^{3}}=\infty}" align="absmiddle" /></li>
</ul>
</li>
	<li> If <img alt="P(x)" src="http://latex.codecogs.com/gif.latex?P(x)" align="absmiddle" /> has a lower power than <img alt="Q(x)" src="http://latex.codecogs.com/gif.latex?Q(x)" align="absmiddle" />, <img alt="\lim_{x \rightarrow \infty }\frac{P(x)}{Q(x)}=0" src="http://latex.codecogs.com/gif.latex?\lim_{x&amp;space;\rightarrow&amp;space;\infty&amp;space;}\frac{P(x)}{Q(x)}=0" align="absmiddle" /> and the line <img alt="y=0" src="http://latex.codecogs.com/gif.latex?y=0" align="absmiddle" /> is a horizontal asymptote on the graph of <img alt="y=\frac{P(x)}{Q(x)}" src="http://latex.codecogs.com/gif.latex?y=\frac{P(x)}{Q(x)}" align="absmiddle" />

<h2 style="text-align: center;">Continuity</h2>

<h3 style="text-align: center;">Kinds of Discontinuities</h3>
<span style="text-decoration: underline; color: #007fff;">Point Discontinuity:</span> Occurs when the limit exits, but the point is defined at another value other than at the limit

<span style="text-decoration: underline; color: #007fff;">Jump Discontinuity:</span> Occurs when the right-hand and left-hand limits exist, but are different
