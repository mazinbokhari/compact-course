<h2 style="text-align: center;"><span style="text-decoration: underline;">Intro to Waves</span></h2>
In the Simple Harmonic Motion chapter, we gained some basic knowledge on waves such as amplitude, period, and frequency. In this chapter, we will further expand on this topic.

<span style="text-decoration: underline; color: #007fff;">Crest:</span> Highest point the wave reaches

<span style="text-decoration: underline;"><span style="color: #007fff; text-decoration: underline;">Trough:</span></span> Lowest point the wave reaches

<span style="text-decoration: underline; color: #007fff;">Wavelength:</span> The distance from one crest to the next (or one trough to the next)

<h2 style="text-align: center;"><span style="text-decoration: underline;">Traveling Waves</span></h2>
<span style="text-decoration: underline;"><span style="color: #007fff; text-decoration: underline;">Traveling Waves:</span></span> Waves that have pulses moving along the length of the string

<a href="http://www.compactcourse.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/traveling-wave.png"><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-4186" style="margin: 5px auto; display: block; text-align: center;" alt="traveling wave" src="http://www.compactcourse.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/traveling-wave.png" width="800" /></a>
<h3 style="text-align: center;">Velocity of a Wave</h3>
The pulse of a wave travel with a constant velocity. This velocity can be given by the equation
<p style="text-align: center;"><img alt="{\color{Blue} v=\lambda f}" src="http://latex.codecogs.com/gif.latex?{\color{Blue}&amp;space;v=\lambda&amp;space;f}" align="absmiddle" /></p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><span style="color: #007fff;">where v is the velocity, λ is the wavelength, and f is the frequency</span></p>

<h3 style="text-align: center;">Velocity of a Wave for Strings</h3>
When someone gives a taught string a quick whip, a pulse develops in the string. The velocity of this pulse is given by the equation
<p style="text-align: center;"><img alt="{\color{Blue} v=\sqrt{\frac{F_{t}}{M/l}}=\sqrt{\frac{F_{t}}{\mu }}}" src="http://latex.codecogs.com/gif.latex?{\color{Blue}&amp;space;v=\sqrt{\frac{F_{t}}{M/l}}=\sqrt{\frac{F_{t}}{\mu&amp;space;}}}" align="absmiddle" /></p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><span style="color: #007fff;">where F<sub>t</sub> is the force of tension in the string, M is the mass of the string, l is the length of the string, and <b>μ is </b>the linear density of the string equal to M/l</span></p>

<h3 style="text-align: center;">Superposition</h3>
<span style="text-decoration: underline;"><span style="color: #007fff; text-decoration: underline;">Superposition:</span></span> When two pulses meet, their resulting amplitude displacement is equal to the sum of the individual displacements

<span style="text-decoration: underline; color: #007fff;">Constructive Interference:</span> When the sum of the displacements creates a bigger pulse

<a href="http://www.compactcourse.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/constructive-21.png"><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-4193" style="margin: 5px auto; display: block; text-align: center;" alt="constructive 2" src="http://www.compactcourse.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/constructive-21.png" width="800" /></a>

<span style="text-decoration: underline; color: #007fff;">Destructive Interference:</span> When the sum of the displacements creates a smaller pulse
<h2 style="text-align: center;"><span style="text-decoration: underline;">Standing Waves</span></h2>
<span style="text-decoration: underline; color: #007fff;">Standing Wave:</span> Fixed wave in which there are no pulses that travel down the string

<span style="text-decoration: underline;"><span style="color: #007fff; text-decoration: underline;">Node (N):</span></span> Point on a wave in which no displacement occurs

<span style="text-decoration: underline;"><span style="color: #007fff; text-decoration: underline;">Antinode (A):</span></span> Point on a wave in which maximum and minimum displacement occur

Nodes and antinodes are all equally spaced from one another

<a href="http://www.compactcourse.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/Nodes.png"><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-4184" style="margin: 5px auto; display: block; text-align: center;" alt="Nodes" src="http://www.compactcourse.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/Nodes.png" width="500" /></a>
