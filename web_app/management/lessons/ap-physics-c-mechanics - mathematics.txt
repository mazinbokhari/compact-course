<h2 style="text-align: center;"><span style="text-decoration: underline;">The Dot Product</span></h2>
<span style="text-decoration: underline;"><span style="color: #3366ff; text-decoration: underline;">Dot Product:</span></span> Scalar quantity that is the magnitude of one vector's component that runs parallel to another vector
<ul>
	<li>The dot product of two vectors can be found through the equation <img class="aligncenter" alt="{\color{Blue} A\cdot B=\left | A \right |\left | B \right |\cos \Theta }" src="http://latex.codecogs.com/gif.latex?{\color{Blue}&amp;space;A\cdot&amp;space;B=\left&amp;space;|&amp;space;A&amp;space;\right&amp;space;|\left&amp;space;|&amp;space;B&amp;space;\right&amp;space;|\cos&amp;space;\Theta&amp;space;}" width="160" /></li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: center;"><span style="color: #007fff;">where |A| and |B| are the magnitudes of the two vectors and Θ is the angle between the two vectors</span></p>

<ul>
	<li>It can also be found with the formula</li>
</ul>
<img class="aligncenter" alt="{\color{Blue} A\cdot B=(A_{x})(B_{x})+(A_{y})(B_{y})+(A_{z})(B_{z}))}" src="http://latex.codecogs.com/gif.latex?{\color{Blue}&amp;space;A\cdot&amp;space;B=(A_{x})(B_{x})+(A_{y})(B_{y})+(A_{z})(B_{z}))}" align="absmiddle" />
<p style="text-align: center;"><span style="color: #007fff;">where A<sub>x</sub>, A<sub>y</sub>, and A<sub>z</sub> are the components of vector A and B<sub>x</sub>, B<sub>y</sub>, and B<sub>z</sub> are the components of vector B</span></p>

<h3 style="text-align: center;">Positive Dot Product</h3>
When Θ is between 0º and 90º, the dot product is positive because the cosine of this angle has to be positive
<p style="text-align: center;"><a href="http://www.compactcourse.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/cross-product-+.png"><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-4321" alt="cross product +" src="http://www.compactcourse.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/cross-product-+.png" width="365" height="311" /></a></p>

<h3 style="text-align: center;">Negative Dot Product</h3>
When Θ is between 90º and 180º, the dot product is negative because the cosine of this angle has to be negative

<a href="http://www.compactcourse.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/cross-product-.png"><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-4320" style="margin: 5px auto; display: block; text-align: center;" alt="cross product -" src="http://www.compactcourse.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/cross-product-.png" width="526" /></a>

&nbsp;
<h3 style="text-align: center;">Dot Product Equal to Zero</h3>
When Θ equals 90º, the dot product is zero because the cosine of this angle has to be zero
<p style="text-align: center;"><a href="http://www.compactcourse.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/cross-product-0.png"><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-4322" alt="cross product 0" src="http://www.compactcourse.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/cross-product-0.png" width="408" height="270" /></a></p>

<h2 style="text-align: center;"><span style="text-decoration: underline;">The Cross Product</span></h2>
<span style="text-decoration: underline;"><span style="color: #3366ff; text-decoration: underline;">Cross Product:</span></span> Vector quantity that is the magnitude of one vector's component that runs parallel to another vector
<ul>
	<li>The <span style="text-decoration: underline;">magnitude</span> of the cross product of two vectors can be found through the equation</li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: center;"><img alt="{\color{Blue} \left |A\times B \right |=\left | A \right |\left | B \right |\sin \Theta }" src="http://latex.codecogs.com/gif.latex?{\color{Blue}&amp;space;\left&amp;space;|A\times&amp;space;B&amp;space;\right&amp;space;|=\left&amp;space;|&amp;space;A&amp;space;\right&amp;space;|\left&amp;space;|&amp;space;B&amp;space;\right&amp;space;|\sin&amp;space;\Theta&amp;space;}" align="absmiddle" /></p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><span style="color: #007fff;">where |A| and |B| are the magnitudes of the two vectors and Θ is the angle between the two vectors</span></p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><a href="http://www.compactcourse.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/cross-product-diagram.png"><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-4325" style="margin: 5px auto; display: block; text-align: center;" alt="cross product diagram" src="http://www.compactcourse.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/cross-product-diagram.png" width="350" /></a></p>

<ul>
	<li>The <span style="text-decoration: underline;">direction</span> of the cross product of two vectors can be found through the equation</li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: center;"><img alt="{\color{Blue} A\times B=(AxBy-AyBx)\widehat{k} + (AyBz-AzBy)\widehat{i }+ (AzBy-AxBz)\widehat{j} }" src="http://latex.codecogs.com/gif.latex?{\color{Blue}&amp;space;A\times&amp;space;B=(AxBy-AyBx)\widehat{k}&amp;space;+&amp;space;(AyBz-AzBy)\widehat{i&amp;space;}+&amp;space;(AzBy-AxBz)\widehat{j}&amp;space;}" align="absmiddle" /></p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><span style="color: #007fff;">where A<sub>x</sub>, A<sub>y</sub>, and A<sub>z</sub> are the components of vector A and B<sub>x</sub>, B<sub>y</sub>, and B<sub>z</sub> are the components of vector B</span></p>

<ul>
	<li>This above formula is the same thing as taking the determinant of the two vectors</li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: center;"><a href="http://www.compactcourse.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/cross-product-2.png"><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-4304" style="margin: 5px auto; display: block; text-align: center;" alt="cross product 2" src="http://www.compactcourse.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/cross-product-2.png" width="400" /></a></p>

<h3 style="text-align: center;">Right Hand Rule for the Cross Product</h3>
To find the direction of the cross product vector,
<ul>
	<li>Use your right hand and stick your fingers in the direction of the first vector</li>
	<li>Face your palm in the direction of the second vector</li>
	<li>Your thumb points in the direction of the cross product vector</li>
</ul>
