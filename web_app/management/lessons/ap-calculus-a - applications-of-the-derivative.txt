<h2 style="text-align: center;">Tangents and Normals to a Curve</h2>
<h3 style="text-align: center;">Tangents</h3>
The tangent line to a curve <img alt="y=f(x)" src="http://latex.codecogs.com/gif.latex?y=f(x)" align="absmiddle" /> at a point <img alt="Q(x_{1},y_{1})" src="http://latex.codecogs.com/gif.latex?Q(x_{1},y_{1})" align="absmiddle" /> can be given by the equation

<img class="aligncenter" alt="{\color{Blue} y-y_{1}={f}'(x)(x-x_{1})}" src="http://latex.codecogs.com/gif.latex?{\color{Blue}&amp;space;y-y_{1}={f}'(x)(x-x_{1})}" align="absmiddle" />
<p style="text-align: center;">because the slope of the tangent line is equal to <img alt="{f}'(x)" src="http://latex.codecogs.com/gif.latex?{f}'(x)" align="absmiddle" /> at that point</p>

<h3 style="text-align: center;">Normals</h3>
The normal line to a curve <img alt="y=f(x)" src="http://latex.codecogs.com/gif.latex?y=f(x)" align="absmiddle" /> at a point <img alt="Q(x_{1},y_{1})" src="http://latex.codecogs.com/gif.latex?Q(x_{1},y_{1})" align="absmiddle" /> can be given by the equation

<img class="aligncenter" alt="{\color{Blue} y-y_{1}=-\frac{1}{{f}'(x)}(x-x_{1})}" src="http://latex.codecogs.com/gif.latex?{\color{Blue}&amp;space;y-y_{1}=-\frac{1}{{f}'(x)}(x-x_{1})}" align="absmiddle" />
<p style="text-align: center;">because the slope of a normal line in the opposite reciprocal of the slope of the tangent line</p>

<h2 style="text-align: center;">Critical Points</h2>
We call a point at <img alt="x=c" src="http://latex.codecogs.com/gif.latex?x=c" align="absmiddle" /> a <span style="color: #007fff;">critical point</span> of a function <img alt="f(x)" src="http://latex.codecogs.com/gif.latex?f(x)" align="absmiddle" /> if <img alt="f(c)" src="http://latex.codecogs.com/gif.latex?f(c)" align="absmiddle" /> exists and if
<p style="padding-left: 30px;">1. <img alt="{f}'(c)=0" src="http://latex.codecogs.com/gif.latex?{f}'(c)=0" align="absmiddle" /></p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><span style="text-decoration: underline;">OR</span></p>
<p style="text-align: left; padding-left: 30px;">2. <img alt="{f}'(c)" src="http://latex.codecogs.com/gif.latex?{f}'(c)" align="absmiddle" /> does not exist</p>

<h3 style="text-align: center;">Finding Critical Points</h3>
Ex.) Find all the critical points on the function <img alt="f(x)=2x^{3}+3x^{2}-12x" src="http://latex.codecogs.com/gif.latex?f(x)=2x^{3}+3x^{2}-12x" align="absmiddle" />
<ul>
	<li><span style="color: #ff0000;">To find the critical points we have to first find the derivative of <img alt="{\color{Red} f(x)}" src="http://latex.codecogs.com/gif.latex?{\color{Red}&amp;space;f(x)}" align="absmiddle" /></span></li>
	<li><span style="color: #ff0000;">This comes out to be <img alt="{\color{Red} {f}'(x)=6x^{2}+6x-12}" src="http://latex.codecogs.com/gif.latex?{\color{Red}&amp;space;{f}'(x)=6x^{2}+6x-12}" align="absmiddle" /></span></li>
	<li><span style="color: #ff0000;">This can be rewritten as <img alt="{\color{Red} {f}'(x)=(3x-3)(2x+4)}" src="http://latex.codecogs.com/gif.latex?{\color{Red}&amp;space;{f}'(x)=(3x-3)(2x+4)}" align="absmiddle" /></span></li>
	<li><span style="color: #ff0000;">Critical points occur when <img alt="{\color{Red} {f}'(c)=0}" src="http://latex.codecogs.com/gif.latex?{\color{Red}&amp;space;{f}'(c)=0}" align="absmiddle" /> so we set <img alt="{\color{Red} {f}'(x)}" src="http://latex.codecogs.com/gif.latex?{\color{Red}&amp;space;{f}'(x)}" align="absmiddle" /> equal to 0 which gives us <img alt="{\color{Red} 0=(3x-3)(2x+4)}" src="http://latex.codecogs.com/gif.latex?{\color{Red}&amp;space;0=(3x-3)(2x+4)}" align="absmiddle" /></span></li>
	<li><span style="color: #ff0000;">This means there are critical points at <img alt="{\color{Red} x= 1}" src="http://latex.codecogs.com/gif.latex?{\color{Red}&amp;space;x=&amp;space;1}" align="absmiddle" /> and at <img alt="{\color{Red} x=-2}" src="http://latex.codecogs.com/gif.latex?{\color{Red}&amp;space;x=-2}" align="absmiddle" /></span></li>
	<li><span style="color: #ff0000;">Critical points also occur where <img alt="{\color{Red} {f}'(x)}" src="http://latex.codecogs.com/gif.latex?{\color{Red}&amp;space;{f}'(x)}" align="absmiddle" /> does not exist, but there are no points on the function <img alt="{\color{Red} {f}'(x)=6x^{2}+6x-12}" src="http://latex.codecogs.com/gif.latex?{\color{Red}&amp;space;{f}'(x)=6x^{2}+6x-12}" align="absmiddle" /> where <img alt="{\color{Red} {f}'(x)}" src="http://latex.codecogs.com/gif.latex?{\color{Red}&amp;space;{f}'(x)}" align="absmiddle" /> does not exist</span></li>
	<li><span style="color: #ff0000;">Therefore the only critical points occur at <img alt="{\color{Red} x= 1}" src="http://latex.codecogs.com/gif.latex?{\color{Red}&amp;space;x=&amp;space;1}" align="absmiddle" /> and at <img alt="{\color{Red} x=-2}" src="http://latex.codecogs.com/gif.latex?{\color{Red}&amp;space;x=-2}" align="absmiddle" /></span></li>
</ul>
Ex.) Find all the critical points on the function <img alt="f(x)=(x-4)^{1/2}" src="http://latex.codecogs.com/gif.latex?f(x)=(x-4)^{1/2}" align="absmiddle" />
<ul>
	<li><span style="color: #ff0000;">To find the critical points we have to first find the derivative of <img alt="{\color{Red} f(x)}" src="http://latex.codecogs.com/gif.latex?{\color{Red}&amp;space;f(x)}" align="absmiddle" /></span></li>
	<li><span style="color: #ff0000;">This comes out to be <img alt="{\color{Red} {f}'(x)=\frac{1}{(x-4)^{1/2}}}" src="http://latex.codecogs.com/gif.latex?{\color{Red}&amp;space;{f}'(x)=\frac{1}{(x-4)^{1/2}}}" align="absmiddle" /></span></li>
	<li><span style="color: #ff0000;">Critical points occur when <img alt="{\color{Red} {f}'(c)=0}" src="http://latex.codecogs.com/gif.latex?{\color{Red}&amp;space;{f}'(c)=0}" align="absmiddle" />, but there are no points here where <img alt="{\color{Red} {f}'(c)=0}" src="http://latex.codecogs.com/gif.latex?{\color{Red}&amp;space;{f}'(c)=0}" align="absmiddle" />. Therefore the only critical points occur where <img alt="{\color{Red} {f}'(c)}" src="http://latex.codecogs.com/gif.latex?{\color{Red}&amp;space;{f}'(c)}" align="absmiddle" /> does not exist</span></li>

</ul>
<h2 style="text-align: center;">Optimization</h2>
We can start to apply what we learned in calculus so far to real life situations through optimization. We look for maximum or minimum values for a function that satisfies a constrain that has to be true no matter what. In a lot of these problems, there is a pattern on how to solve them. These steps are seen below
<ol>
	<li><span style="line-height: 14px;">Identify the constraint and the value to be optimized</span></li>
	<li>Rearrange the constraint equation to solve for one of the variables</li>
	<li>Plug this into the equation that needs to be optimized</li>
	<li>Differentiate this equation and set it equal to 0</li>
	<li>Solve for the values that make it equal to 0</li>
	<li>Choose the value that is the maximum or minimum that we are looking for</li>
	<li>Solve for the other variables</li>
</ol>
<p style="padding-left: 30px;">ex.) A gardener has 300 feet of fencing to enclose three equal sized rectangular garden plots. What dimensions x and y will maximize the total area?</p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><a href="http://www.compactcourse.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/Untitled.png"><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-5592" alt="Untitled" src="http://www.compactcourse.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/Untitled.png" width="485" height="266" /></a></p>

<ul>
	<li><span style="color: #ff0000;">The constraint equation is <img alt="{\color{Red} 2x+4y=300}" src="http://latex.codecogs.com/gif.latex?{\color{Red}&amp;space;2x+4y=300}" align="absmiddle" /> and the optimization equation is <img alt="{\color{Red} Total\, \, Area = xy}" src="http://latex.codecogs.com/gif.latex?{\color{Red}&amp;space;Total\,&amp;space;\,&amp;space;Area&amp;space;=&amp;space;xy}" align="absmiddle" />.</span></li>
	<li><span style="color: #ff0000;">We can rearrange the constraint equation to solve for y</span>
<ul>
	<li><img alt="{\color{Red} 2x+4y=300}" src="http://latex.codecogs.com/gif.latex?{\color{Red}&amp;space;2x+4y=300}" align="absmiddle" /></li>
	<li><img alt="{\color{Red} 4y=300-2x}" src="http://latex.codecogs.com/gif.latex?{\color{Red}&amp;space;4y=300-2x}" align="absmiddle" /></li>
	<li><img alt="{\color{Red} y=\frac{300}{4}-\frac{2x}{4}}" src="http://latex.codecogs.com/gif.latex?{\color{Red}&amp;space;y=\frac{300}{4}-\frac{2x}{4}}" align="absmiddle" /></li>
	<li><img alt="{\color{Red} y=75-\frac{x}{2}}" src="http://latex.codecogs.com/gif.latex?{\color{Red}&amp;space;y=75-\frac{x}{2}}" align="absmiddle" /></li>
</ul>
</li>
	<li><span style="color: #ff0000;">Plug y into the optimization equation to get <img alt="{\color{Red} Total \, \, Area=x\left (75-\frac{x}{2} \right )=75x-\frac{x^{2}}{2}}" src="http://latex.codecogs.com/gif.latex?{\color{Red}&amp;space;Total&amp;space;\,&amp;space;\,&amp;space;Area=x\left&amp;space;(75-\frac{x}{2}&amp;space;\right&amp;space;)=75x-\frac{x^{2}}{2}}" align="absmiddle" /></span></li>
	<li><span style="color: #ff0000;">Differentiate this to get <img alt="{\color{Red} \left (Total \, \, Area \right ){}'(x)=75-x}" src="http://latex.codecogs.com/gif.latex?{\color{Red}&amp;space;\left&amp;space;(Total&amp;space;\,&amp;space;\,&amp;space;Area&amp;space;\right&amp;space;){}'(x)=75-x}" align="absmiddle" /> and set it equal to 0 to get <img alt="{\color{Red} 0=75-x}" src="http://latex.codecogs.com/gif.latex?{\color{Red}&amp;space;0=75-x}" align="absmiddle" /></span></li>
	<li><span style="color: #ff0000;">So <img alt="{\color{Red} x=75}" src="http://latex.codecogs.com/gif.latex?{\color{Red}&amp;space;x=75}" align="absmiddle" /></span></li>
	<li><span style="color: #ff0000;">Plugging this into <img alt="{\color{Red} 2x+4y=300}" src="http://latex.codecogs.com/gif.latex?{\color{Red}&amp;space;2x+4y=300}" align="absmiddle" />, we get <img alt="{\color{Red} 2(75)+4y=300}" src="http://latex.codecogs.com/gif.latex?{\color{Red}&amp;space;2(75)+4y=300}" align="absmiddle" /> which means <img alt="{\color{Red} y=37.5}" src="http://latex.codecogs.com/gif.latex?{\color{Red}&amp;space;y=37.5}" align="absmiddle" /></span></li>
	<li><span style="color: #ff0000;">Therefore our optimal dimensions are <img alt="{\color{Red} x=75}" src="http://latex.codecogs.com/gif.latex?{\color{Red}&amp;space;x=75}" align="absmiddle" /> and <img alt="{\color{Red} y=37.5}" src="http://latex.codecogs.com/gif.latex?{\color{Red}&amp;space;y=37.5}" align="absmiddle" /></span></li>
