<h2 style="text-align: center;"> <span style="text-decoration: underline; color: #007fff;">Current</span></h2>
<span style="text-decoration: underline;"><span style="color: #007fff; text-decoration: underline;">Current:</span></span> Charge that moves through a conductor over a certain time period
<ul>
	<li>Units =&gt; Ampere <img alt="(A)" src="http://latex.codecogs.com/gif.latex?(A)" align="absmiddle" /> Symbol =&gt; I</li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: center;"><img alt="{\color{Blue} I=\frac{\Delta Q}{\Delta t}}" src="http://latex.codecogs.com/gif.latex?{\color{Blue}&amp;space;I=\frac{\Delta&amp;space;Q}{\Delta&amp;space;t}}" align="absmiddle" /></p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><span style="color: #007fff;">where ΔQ is the charge that moves through in a time, t</span></p>

<h2 style="text-align: center;"><span style="text-decoration: underline; color: #007fff;">Resistance</span></h2>
<span style="text-decoration: underline; color: #007fff;">Resistance:</span> Electrical friction within a conductor when current passes through it
<ul>
	<li>Units =&gt; Ohm <img alt="(\Omega )" src="http://latex.codecogs.com/gif.latex?(\Omega&amp;space;)" align="absmiddle" /> Symbol =&gt; R</li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: center;"><img alt="{\color{Blue} R=\frac{V}{I}}" src="http://latex.codecogs.com/gif.latex?{\color{Blue}&amp;space;R=\frac{V}{I}}" align="absmiddle" /> which means <img alt="{\color{Blue} V=IR}" src="http://latex.codecogs.com/gif.latex?{\color{Blue}&amp;space;V=IR}" align="absmiddle" /></p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><span style="color: #007fff;">where V is the voltage and I is the current through the conductor</span></p>

<h3 style="text-align: center;"><span style="color: #007fff;">Resistivity</span></h3>
The amount of resistance that occurs within an object depends on its properties. The resistance is found using the equation below
<p style="text-align: center;"><img alt="{\color{Blue} R=\frac{\rho L}{A}}" src="http://latex.codecogs.com/gif.latex?{\color{Blue}&amp;space;R=\frac{\rho&amp;space;L}{A}}" align="absmiddle" /></p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><span style="color: #007fff;">where A is the cross-sectional area of the object, L is the length, and ρ is the resistivity constant, which varies for different elements and will be given in most problems</span></p>

<h2 style="text-align: center;"><span style="text-decoration: underline; color: #007fff;">Kirchoff's Rules</span></h2>
<h3 style="text-align: center;"><span style="color: #007fff;">Loop Rule</span></h3>
<p style="text-align: center;">The sum of the positive and negative potentials around any closed loop is equal to 0</p>

<h3 style="text-align: center;"><span style="color: #007fff;">Junction Rule</span></h3>
<p style="text-align: center;"> The amount of current that enters a branch is equal to the current leaving it</p>

<h2 style="text-align: center;"><span style="text-decoration: underline;"><span style="color: #007fff; text-decoration: underline;">Power in Circuits</span></span></h2>
The power outputted by a circuit can be given by the equation
<p style="text-align: center;"><img alt="{\color{Blue} P=VI=I^2R=\frac{V^2}{R}}" src="http://latex.codecogs.com/gif.latex?{\color{Blue}&amp;space;P=VI=I^2R=\frac{V^2}{R}}" align="absmiddle" /></p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><span style="color: #007fff;">where R is the resistance, V is the voltage, and I is the current</span></p>
<p style="text-align: center;">*Keep the previous power equation P = W / t in mind for certain problems.*</p>
